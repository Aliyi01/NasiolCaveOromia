About Nasiol Cave

Nasiol Cave is a breathtaking underground natural cave located in Tokkuma Bilisummaa village, Melka Belo district of Eastern Ethiopia. It was discovered by cave specialist Nasir Ahmad and is celebrated as the second largest cave in the world and the largest in Africa. A marvel of nature, Nasiol Cave attracts adventurers, geologists, and tourists seeking raw beauty and subterranean wonder.

How to Get There

Nasiol Cave is located in Tokkuma Bilisummaa village, Melka Belo district, Eastern Ethiopia.
The nearest major city is Dire Dawa (approx. 5-hour drive). Visitors can reach the cave by road via local transport or private tours.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal visiting period is during the dry season (October to March) when roads are accessible and the cave environment is safest and most enjoyable.

Local Accommodations

Nearby towns offer guesthouses and lodges. Visitors can also stay in Dire Dawa for a wider range of hotels and easy access to the cave.

Safety Guidelines

Visitors should join guided tours to navigate the cave safely.
Wear sturdy footwear, bring a flashlight, and be cautious of slippery or uneven surfaces inside the cave.
